# TECH: Persona template + good vs bad examples

## What it does

Persona-authoring structure for the Define phase. Personas must be grounded in real research data; aspirational or demographic-only personas are worse than no personas because they encode bias.

## When to use

After running 5-8+ user interviews and clustering observations. The output is 3-5 personas per product — enough to represent distinct needs, few enough to remember by name.

## How it works

Six elements, in this order:

1. **Name + photo** — makes persona memorable. Names humans name humans. Avoid "User A" and stock-photo faces with "OMG I'm drinking coffee" expressions.
2. **Goals** — functional ("order groceries in 10 min") + emotional ("feel in control").
3. **Pain points** — specific frustrations with current solutions. Must be observable, not aspirational.
4. **Behaviors** — how they currently solve the problem. Evidence, not fiction.
5. **Context** — when, where, why they use the product.
6. **Quote** — single representative sentence capturing the mindset.

## Minimal example

### Good persona

```markdown
## Sarah, The Busy Parent
Age 34 · Marketing Manager · Austin, TX

### Goals
- Order groceries in under 10 min during her commute
- Never run out of diapers, milk, snacks

### Pain Points
- Scrolling past irrelevant promos to find basics
- Forgets reorder because it's buried

### Behaviors
- Shops on phone, never desktop
- Reorders 70% of the same items weekly

### Context
- Uses commute window (9-9:15 am) or lunch break (12:30 pm)
- One-handed, often interrupted

> "I just need the basics fast. Don't make me think about groceries more than I have to."
```

### Bad persona

```markdown
## User Type A
Age 25-45 · Gender: Any · Income: Middle class
Goals: Wants a good experience. Likes easy-to-use apps.
[Too vague to inform any design decision]
```

## Gotchas

- Demographic-only personas ("25-45, middle income") describe a census bracket, not a user archetype. They drive no design decision.
- The quote must come from an actual interview. Fabricated quotes ("I love using simple apps!") poison the persona.
- 3-5 personas is the ceiling. 7+ personas means no one will remember them and they stop being referenced.
- Update personas every 6-12 months — user needs drift as the market changes.
